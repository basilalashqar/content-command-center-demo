AI Content Command Center

A controlled content operating workflow for Qatar Living's Events, News & Social team — not a generic AI writer. The system detects the opportunity, prepares the content, reviews governance, routes it for human approval, then measures and learns. AI prepares the work; humans approve every decision.

What this system proves

  • It writes in the Qatar Living voice. The brand model is derived from a study of 1,999 published articles: entity-first headlines, attribution-heavy prose, the institutional stance verbs the newsroom actually uses.
  • It enforces governance automatically. 46.5% of the analysed content needs senior review; the system routes those items to humans by default and never auto-publishes.
  • It tidies copy at publish time. The same boilerplate CTA paragraph was detected in ~1,425 articles, alongside paste artefacts and stray markup that hurt SEO, accessibility and machine-readability.
  • It surfaces the event-freshness problem on day one. 57.6% of listed events had already ended at study time (80.5% today); the system flags every one with a suggested correction for approval.

Persona summary

Institutional, formal, optimistic, ceremonial, attribution-heavy. Entity-first headlines. Stance verbs: announced, launched, stressed, affirmed, welcomed, signed. Honorifics respected (29% of articles). Avoids opinion, clickbait, and sensational language.
